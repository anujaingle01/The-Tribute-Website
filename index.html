Dr. B. R. Ambedkar

1891 - 1956

Father of the Indian Constitution

Dr. B. R. Ambedkar, born on April 14, 1891, was a visionary leader, social reformer, and jurist who dedicated his life to the fight against social discrimination and injustice. As the chief architect of the Indian Constitution, he played a monumental role in shaping modern India's democratic framework. Ambedkar tirelessly advocated for the rights and empowerment of marginalized communities, particularly Dalits, also known as untouchables. His relentless efforts led to the inclusion of provisions for social equality, freedom, and justice in the Constitution. Beyond his legislative contributions, Ambedkar was a prolific writer and scholar, addressing issues of caste, religion, and human rights. His legacy continues to inspire generations, symbolizing the struggle for social justice and equality worldwide.

Biographies

  • Born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow, India.
  • Fought against caste-based discrimination and inequality.
  • Chief architect of the Indian Constitution.
  • First Law Minister of independent India.
  • Pioneered efforts for social and economic justice.
  • Founded the Independent Labour Party to represent the Dalit community.
  • Advocated for affirmative action and reservations for marginalized groups.
  • His teachings and philosophy continue to influence social reform movements globally.
